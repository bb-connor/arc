## 1. Top 5 Insights
- Inference: The current wedge is too broad. The strongest entry point inside it is `model/policy change review and release certification`, not the full bundle of approvals, overrides, exceptions, and change reviews. The clearest official control hooks are around documented approval, testing, validation, periodic review, and change logs, not around daily approval evidence as a standalone category.
- Inference: The most credible day-one champion is the owner of controlled rollout in the first line: head of electronic trading platform, algorithm controls, or workflow engineering. Compliance, model risk, and security are critical gatekeepers, but the economic buyer is more likely the business-line COO, head of e-trading, or markets CTO than a generic compliance lead.
- Inference: Early value is operational, not cryptographic. FINRA, the Fed, SEC, and MiFID-style controls all point to documented governance, validation, inventory, and reviewability. MERCURY should therefore lead with “faster complete review packs and reconstruction,” while signed proofs remain the differentiator in diligence, audit, and external dispute contexts.
- Inference: Third-party AI is a stronger pain signal than the docs currently emphasize. The BoE/FCA found one-third of AI use cases are third-party, 46% of firms report only partial understanding of their AI, 84% have an accountable AI owner, and 87% cite change-management practices. That makes vendor/model intake plus change evidence a sharper initial workflow than generic approval trace.
- Inference: MERCURY should avoid drifting into generic AI governance. Credo AI and ModelOp already market AI registry, policy enforcement, approval workflows, and audit-ready documentation. MERCURY wins only if it stays capital-markets-specific: decision lineage, retained artifacts, downstream reconciliation, and independently verifiable proof packs for market workflows.

## 2. Top 3 Risks
- Inference: The current positioning risks creating no clean budget line. FINRA is explicit that its AI notice is technology-neutral and creates no new requirements; if MERCURY is sold as a newly mandated evidence category, buyers can delay, redirect to existing controls, or self-build.
- Inference: Combining approval, override, exception review, shadow review, and change review in the first wedge spreads product and GTM across too many workflows, owners, and systems. That makes pilots likely to look like bespoke consulting.
- Inference: The current packaging likely asks buyers to pay for infrastructure before they feel a workflow delta. A `45-day pilot` and `$200K-$500K` annual land price work only if proof of value is tied to hard workflow KPIs like review-cycle time, documentation completeness, and investigation speed.

## 3. Top 3 Better Ideas
- Inference: **Best idea: AI Change Review / Release Certification.** Product: system of record for one governed model, policy, or workflow change, with required artifacts, replay or shadow comparison, sign-off, and proof pack. Buyer: head of electronic trading platform or algo controls; gatekeepers: model risk, compliance, security. Packaging: `2-week workflow mapping sprint` at `$25K-$40K`, creditable into a `6-8 week pilot` at `$75K-$125K`; annual land at `$150K-$300K` per governed program. Proof of value: time to assemble review pack, time to approve or rollback, share of material changes with complete evidence, and readiness for periodic validation.
- Inference: **Second-best idea: Incident Reconstruction / Inquiry Pack.** Product: one-click reconstruction of a disputed AI-assisted workflow with model version, policy snapshot, chronology, approvals, artifacts, and downstream IDs. Buyer: platform COO, surveillance lead, or compliance ops. Packaging: pilot on one incident class or workflow family; annual module at roughly `$100K-$250K` if it becomes the standard investigation substrate. Proof of value: time to reconstruct, number of systems queried per case, and time to answer internal, client, or audit inquiries.
- Inference: **Third idea: Third-Party AI Intake and Ongoing Review for Markets Workflows.** Product: capital-markets-specific intake, approval, evidence capture, version tracking, and periodic review for external AI tools or embedded vendor models touching trading workflows. Buyer: architecture or AI governance plus procurement and control partners. Packaging: better as an expansion from idea 1 than as the first product, because generic AI governance vendors already own much of this category. Proof of value: inventory completeness, time to onboard a vendor model, and share of third-party AI use cases with named owner, policy mapping, and change history.
