## 1. Top 5 Insights
- The core framing is mostly right: MERCURY is positioned as decision-provenance / supervisory-evidence infrastructure, not as a turnkey compliance platform or best-execution proof. That aligns with SEC, FINRA, and Federal Reserve source material.
- The biggest remaining gap is regime specificity. The docs still mix broker-dealer, investment-adviser, and bank-model-risk concepts without a clear matrix of which rules, records, approvals, and disclosures apply to which customer type.
- Books-and-records is still framed too abstractly. SEC/FINRA sources focus on record format, accessibility, prompt furnishing, audit-trail/WORM profile, and supervision/retention of actual business communications, not just tamper-evident provenance.
- Inference from SEC/FINRA sources: MERCURY needs to preserve the exact content actually sent or reviewed, not only hashes, manifests, or dynamically regenerated exports. That matters for proof packages, linked artifacts, prompts/outputs, and any external explanation to clients.
- Model governance and live-control are under-specified operationally. FINRA’s 2026 GenAI guidance and SR 11-7 point beyond version IDs into prompt/output logging, validation, monitoring, documentation, and human review; if MERCURY ever mediates live actions, 15c3-5-style market-access controls become central.

## 2. Top 3 Risks
- External overclaim risk. Current “assurance” and client-facing language can be heard as “approved disclosure channel” or “records solution” before communications review, substantiation, and retention controls exist.
- Recordkeeping failure risk. The roadmap mentions record taxonomy, legal hold, and prompt production, but it does not yet require preservation of the final sent package, linked/embedded materials, recipient log, or a passed production drill.
- Live-path risk. If “supervised live” or “mediated in-line control” expands without direct/exclusive control, threshold governance, vendor oversight, testing, and annual review artifacts, MERCURY could create new scrutiny rather than reduce it.

## 3. Top 3 Missing Controls
- Records + legal-hold orchestration: per artifact, capture governing regime, record class, retention trigger, retention period, audit-trail/WORM mode, readily-accessible window, hold-matter ID, system-of-record destination, and production format/SLA.
- Communications/disclosure gate: classify each export as internal, institutional, retail, or regulator-facing; require approver identity, substantiation evidence, final rendered snapshot, recipient/delivery log, and retention of any linked or embedded content.
- Model / live-control governance: bind model inventory IDs, prompt/output logs, model/version lineage, validation status, monitoring metrics, human-review checkpoints, threshold-change logs, and annual effectiveness review evidence into the MERCURY record chain.
