## Wave 1 Question

After the current roadmap and the current addendum phases are complete, what
else is required to honestly say ARC has achieved the strongest version of the
project vision?

## Wave 1 Consensus

The first-wave consensus was strong:

1. Phases 21 through 32 already absorb the remaining repo-solvable product and
   protocol closure.
2. The honest remainder after Phase 32 is not another normal engineering phase.
3. What remains after Phase 32 is external proof, partner dependence, operator
   adoption, standards legitimacy, insurance and market validation.
4. ZK receipt proofs and TEE-backed execution should remain explicit research
   tracks, not hidden product milestones.

In short: the roadmap can keep extending through technical closure, but the
vision cannot be fully closed by repository work alone.

## Boundary Interpretation

The practical boundary from Wave 1 is:

- Phases 21 through 25: tighten truth and stop current runtime, release, and
  economic claim drift.
- Phases 26 through 31: close the remaining in-repo strategic gaps around
  provenance, runtime assurance, transparency, spend truth, trust portability,
  and verified-core discipline.
- Phase 32: the boundary where ARC stops being only a repository-deliverable
  system and must be shown as a relied-on external control plane.

Wave 1 did not support adding a normal `Phase 33`.

## Recommended Shape After Phase 32

If the project wants structure after Phase 32, Wave 1 recommends a
non-numbered umbrella program rather than more roadmap phases.

Suggested umbrella:

- `External Proof And Market Validation Program`

Suggested workstreams inside that umbrella:

- operator reliance and bilateral dependence evidence
- partner dependence and real trust-portable integrations
- standards and profile ratification
- market rails, insurance, and marketplace liquidity evidence
- external proof bundles showing that ARC is relied on as control-plane truth,
  not merely integrated as optional software

These workstreams are intentionally not framed as normal product phases because
their exit criteria depend on outside actors, not only repository changes.

## Research Tracks

Wave 1 kept the existing research split:

- ZK receipt proofs
- TEE-backed execution and receipt binding

These remain worthwhile, but they should stay separate from the main execution
path unless the project explicitly decides to turn research into product scope.

## What Wave 1 Rejected

Wave 1 rejected the idea that the repo still needs more ordinary roadmap phases
after Phase 32 to complete the vision honestly.

Wave 1 also rejected reopening provenance, attestation, transparency, and
identity as fresh post-32 phase families, because those concerns are already
accounted for inside Phases 26 through 31.

## Open Tension To Test In Wave 2

Wave 2 should pressure-test four points:

1. Whether Phase 32 should remain the final numbered phase.
2. Whether the post-32 remainder should be one umbrella program or several
   named external programs.
3. Whether any part of the market-validation story still belongs inside the
   addendum rather than in a separate companion doc.
4. Whether the current addendum cleanly distinguishes repo-provable closure
   from external evidence and research.

## Bottom Line

Wave 1 says the roadmap should not pretend that the strongest ARC thesis can be
completed by internal implementation alone.

The numbered roadmap can plausibly run through Phase 32.
After that, the honest remainder is external proof and research, not another
ordinary product ladder.
