## Executive Summary

ISO/IEC 42001:2023 is the management-system standard for artificial intelligence. It follows the Annex SL high-level structure shared with ISO 27001 and ISO 9001: Context, Leadership, Planning, Support, Operation, Performance Evaluation, Improvement. The AI-specific surface is concentrated in Annex A (the reference control set). ISO 42001 requires a certified organization to establish, operate, and continually improve an AI management system; it is therefore primarily organizational. Chio contributes the technical controls, evidence, and automation that sit under such a management system.

Chio's strongest ISO 42001 contributions are to clause 7.5 (documented information), clause 8.2 and 8.3 (operational planning and AI system impact controls), clause 9.1 (monitoring and measurement), and several Annex A controls, particularly A.7 (AI system logging) and A.5 (data management). Every tool invocation processed by the Chio kernel produces a signed `ChioReceipt` committed to a Merkle checkpoint; retention and archival are configurable; capability scoping and delegation implement least-privilege access to third-party AI components; `chio-underwriting` contributes to impact assessment; PII-oriented data-layer guards contribute to data management.

Clauses 4 (context), 5 (leadership), most of 6 (planning), 7.1 through 7.4 (resources, competence, awareness, communication), and 10 (improvement) are largely organizational and are marked customer-responsibility in this mapping. This document is intended as input to a Statement of Applicability rather than as a certification artifact on its own.

## Gaps Summary

Clauses and Annex A controls where Chio does not fully cover the requirement:

1. **Most of clauses 4, 5, 7.1 to 7.3, 10.** Organizational context, leadership, competence, awareness, improvement. Chio cannot implement these.
2. **Clause 6.1.4 impact assessment artifact format.** `chio-underwriting` produces reasons but not a full impact-assessment report.
3. **Clause 9.3 management review.** Evidence is exportable; the meeting and minutes are organizational.
4. **Annex A.7.3 Data quality and A.7.5 Data preparation.** Out of scope for a tool-governance protocol.
5. **Annex A.6.4 Verification and validation of AI behavior.** Chio verifies protocol behavior, not model behavior.
6. **Continuous-improvement feedback loop (10.1).** Risk-to-policy feedback is tracked as future work (proposed `chio-risk` crate).

Customer-responsibility items:

- AIMS manual, scope statement, risk-assessment methodology, training, awareness, management-review minutes, CAPA program, AI policy prose, stakeholder register, data-quality program.
