# MERCURY Competitive Landscape

**Date:** 2026-04-02  
**Audience:** Strategy, product, investors, and commercial leads

---

## 1. Category Definition

MERCURY occupies the neutral evidence contract layer for governed AI trading
workflows.

The product is differentiated by the combination of:

- signed workflow records
- retained evidence bundles
- checkpoint publication
- independent verification
- trading-specific workflow and reconciliation context

The market is adjacent to several established categories, but none of them is
built around that full evidence contract plus Chio's portable trust substrate.

---

## 2. Adjacent Categories

### Archive and communications compliance

Archive and communications-compliance vendors capture records, retention state,
and supervisory review flows.

Relationship to MERCURY:

- closest category absorber if MERCURY is framed too broadly
- complementary when MERCURY stays workflow-native and proof-native

### TCA

TCA vendors analyze execution outcomes and benchmarking. They remain important
to buyers, but they are outcome-centric rather than decision-provenance-centric.

Relationship to MERCURY:

- complementary
- strong downstream consumer of MERCURY evidence

### Surveillance and case management

Surveillance platforms detect suspicious patterns and manage investigations.
They are natural consumers of MERCURY evidence because decision provenance
strengthens case context.

Relationship to MERCURY:

- complementary today
- potentially competitive if a surveillance vendor builds native attestation

### LLM observability and governance

These products capture prompts, traces, and model behavior. They are closer to
MERCURY than TCA or surveillance, but they are generally optimized for
engineering visibility rather than signed, portable workflow evidence.

Relationship to MERCURY:

- complementary for engineering
- most likely adjacent category to converge technically

### OMS / EMS and execution platforms

OMS and EMS platforms own workflow execution, routing, and order management.
They have distribution and installed base but are not built around portable
evidence publication.

Relationship to MERCURY:

- integration partner first
- feature-risk category second

### Market-data providers

They improve the provenance of market-context artifacts but do not solve the
workflow evidence problem on their own.

Relationship to MERCURY:

- complementary
- strategic if provider-backed provenance becomes important to buyers

### Horizontal AI control towers and governance suites

Horizontal AI governance platforms increasingly cover policy, inventory,
monitoring, and approval workflows across many domains.

Relationship to MERCURY:

- adjacent and potentially competitive in broad governance stories
- not focused on trading-specific causal evidence or neutral verifier exports

---

## 3. White Space

The key gap is not generic AI governance and it is not generic trading
analytics. The gap is:

> portable, signed, inquiry-grade evidence for governed AI trading workflows.

That gap exists because:

- workflow traces alone are weak evidence
- execution analytics alone do not explain the decision chain
- surveillance data alone is too downstream
- archive systems retain records but do not create trading-native proof
- in-house logging rarely has strong publication or verification discipline

---

## 4. Why Buyers Choose MERCURY

Buyers should choose MERCURY when they need:

- stronger reconstruction of a governed change or workflow decision
- retained evidence tied to the signed record
- a proof or inquiry package they can share outside the runtime that created it
- an inquiry package that survives archive, review, and disclosure workflows
- a product that fits between existing systems rather than replacing all of
  them

---

## 5. Competitive Risks

### Feature absorption

Archive, surveillance, governance, or OMS/EMS vendors could add some evidence
features over time.

### Self-build

The most sophisticated firms can build internal versions of parts of the
product, especially if the product story is too vague.

### Distribution asymmetry

Incumbents already own buyer relationships. MERCURY needs strong product focus
and credible pilot outcomes to overcome that.

---

## 6. Defensibility

MERCURY's defensibility comes from:

- reuse of Chio's trust and verification substrate
- trading-specific causal evidence and reconciliation
- practical verifier and inquiry packaging
- credibility created by explicit proof boundaries

The moat is strongest when the product is operationally useful, not when it is
pitched as abstract compliance infrastructure.

---

## Summary

MERCURY is neither just another workflow log product nor a replacement for
existing trading controls. It occupies a distinct evidence layer that adjacent
categories only partially address today, especially when the need is governed
change review, incident reconstruction, and portable inquiry exports.
