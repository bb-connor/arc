# MERCURY Partnership and Integration Strategy

**Date:** 2026-04-03  
**Audience:** Founders, product, BD, and integration leads

---

## 1. Strategy Principles

Partnerships should help MERCURY do one of three things:

- close the next pilot or annual platform deal
- strengthen downstream consumption of MERCURY evidence
- support a justified expansion or integration path

Partnerships should not create speculative integration programs without a clear
buyer or workflow pull.

---

## 2. Priority Order

### Priority 1: design partners

The highest-value relationships are firms willing to run a real pilot and
become reference accounts.

Target profiles:

- bank electronic trading platform teams
- broker-dealer workflow and control-program teams
- selective systematic firms with formal governed rollout workflows

### Priority 2: downstream consumers

The next priority is systems that make MERCURY evidence more useful after it is
created, such as:

- archive and communications-compliance systems
- surveillance platforms
- workflow-governance tools
- review and case-management systems

The first validated choice inside this category was one case-management review
consumer path. The governance-workbench path is now also validated, and the
assurance-suite reviewer family is now complete. The embedded OEM path is also
validated, and the trust-network lane is now complete. The active `v2.50`
choice moves into one Chio-Wall buyer path for a barrier-control-room sponsor.
That keeps the next expansion commercially concrete and avoids opening several
speculative connector, orchestration, portal, trust-broker, or barrier-
platform programs at once.

### Priority 3: supervised-live and production partners

OMS/EMS, FIX, and market-data partners matter when a funded customer wants the
same controlled workflow to move into supervised-live production.

### Priority 4: OEM and embedded-distribution partners

Archive, surveillance, workflow-governance, and review platforms matter once a
customer or late-stage prospect requires MERCURY evidence inside an existing
system of work.

---

## 3. Partner Categories

### Governance and review platforms

Why they matter:

- they already serve compliance and risk buyers
- MERCURY evidence strengthens their cases and investigations

Current selected path:

- one bounded Chio-Wall lane over Chio guard, receipt, checkpoint, publication,
  and verification artifacts
- one buyer motion: `control_room_barrier_review`
- one control surface: `tool_access_domain_boundary`
- one domain pair: `research -> execution`
- one owner boundary: `barrier-control-room` plus `chio-wall-ops`

What to seek:

- adoption of one bounded shared review bundle
- co-selling into shared accounts
- review workflows that do not require Mercury to become a generic portal,
  workflow engine, trust broker, or SDK platform

### OMS / EMS platforms

Why they matter:

- they can reduce friction for funded production deployments

What to seek:

- one targeted adapter path
- test or certification access where commercially justified

### Market-data providers

Why they matter:

- they can improve provenance of market-context artifacts for advanced buyers

What to seek:

- cooperation on timestamping or signed artifacts only when buyer demand exists

### Advisors, consultants, and law firms

Why they matter:

- they can help buyers evaluate the product correctly
- they reduce the risk of misframing MERCURY as a turnkey compliance claim

What to seek:

- education, not resale dependency

### Standards and ecosystem bodies

Why they matter:

- they help shape interoperability and terminology over time

What to seek:

- visibility and vocabulary alignment after product/market signal exists

---

## 4. Integration Policy

Use this rule set for integration decisions:

1. build the next integration only when it is attached to a real commercial
   path
2. prefer downstream evidence consumers before deep runtime coupling
3. prefer supervised-live productionization before embedded OEM packaging
4. prefer embedded OEM packaging before trust-network sharing
5. keep one active expansion or integration program at a time
6. require a named owner for production, embedded, or trust-network complexity

---

## 5. Near-Term Partner Targets

### Best fit now

- prospective design partners
- workflow-governance, review, or case-management platforms
- a small set of trusted advisors with regulated-workflow credibility

### Pull-driven next layer

- one supervised-live workflow partner path tied to a funded account
- one OMS/EMS platform tied to a funded account
- one market-data provenance discussion tied to a real buyer need

---

## 6. Risks

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|
| Too many speculative integrations | engineering focus collapses | tie partner work to pipeline or production ownership |
| Partner sees MERCURY as a competitor | relationship stalls | frame MERCURY as evidence infrastructure, not replacement workflow software |
| Big-name partner pursuit delays shipping | product momentum drops | prioritize deployable relationships over logo hunting |

---

## Summary

The right partner strategy is simple: win design partners first, prove one
downstream evidence-consumer lane second, deepen one governance-workbench
workflow third, deepen one assurance-suite reviewer family fourth, add one
embedded OEM path fifth, and add trust-network sharing only where a specific
sponsor clearly funds it.
