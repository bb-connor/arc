# MERCURY Market Sizing and Financial Model

**Date:** 2026-04-02  
**Audience:** Investors, founders, and strategy leads

---

## 1. Framing Assumptions

MERCURY has a two-layer market:

- a near-term governed-change and inquiry wedge
- a broader platform expansion opportunity

The near-term market is the subset of trading firms that already have AI
materially influencing workflow decisions and care enough about review,
supervision, or client evidence to pay for a product.

---

## 2. Near-Term Evidence Platform Market

### Buyer universe

The realistic near-term universe is approximately **25-50 firms** over the next
24 months across:

- bank electronic trading platform teams
- broker-dealer workflow and control-program teams
- selective systematic firms with formal governed AI rollout

### Contract value

| Offer | Typical value |
|------|---------------|
| Paid pilot | $75K-$150K |
| Annual evidence platform | $150K-$300K |
| Expanded deployment | $400K-$800K+ |

### Serviceable available market

| Segment | Serviceable firms | Typical ACV | SAM contribution |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|------------------|
| Bank platform and workflow teams | 10-20 | $200K-$350K | $2M-$7M |
| Broker-dealer control-program teams | 8-15 | $175K-$300K | $1.4M-$4.5M |
| Selective systematic firms | 7-15 | $150K-$300K | $1.05M-$4.5M |
| **Near-term SAM** | **25-50** |  | **$4.45M-$16M** |

---

## 3. Expanded Platform Opportunity

If the evidence platform lands, the opportunity expands through:

1. more workflows per account
2. supervised-live productionization
3. governance, downstream-consumer, and assurance products
4. embedded distribution, trust-network services, and companion products such
   as Chio-Wall

That broader platform supports a substantially larger business than the initial
evidence wedge alone. A plausible long-range platform range is
**$100M-$300M+ ARR** if the company lands the wedge and expands successfully.

## 5. Pricing Sensitivity

### Floor

Below roughly **$100K annualized**, procurement effort starts to dominate deal
economics for both buyer and vendor.

### Sweet spot

The likely early sweet spot is **$150K-$300K** annualized for one meaningful
controlled workflow or control-program deployment.

### Ceiling

At **$1M+** too early in the product lifecycle, build-vs-buy pressure becomes
material unless the product already supports a mission-critical workflow.

---

## 6. Unit-Economic Profile

The near-term business is not low-touch SaaS.

Expected characteristics:

- founder-assisted selling
- services in early deployments
- longer sales cycles
- concentrated account value

Why it can still work:

- strong expansion potential inside landed accounts
- low marginal compute and storage cost relative to ACV
- high stickiness once evidence workflows become part of review operations

---

## 7. Key Assumptions

- enough firms already feel the governed-change and inquiry gap to pay for a
  pilot
- the product can be delivered as software, not only consulting
- at least some accounts expand after the initial pilot
- buyers value verifiable evidence enough to budget for it explicitly

---

## 8. Key Risks

- the market may still be early for anything beyond shadow-mode evidence
- some firms may accept ordinary logging plus existing controls
- the product could get dragged into custom integrations too early
- the most sophisticated firms may self-build instead of buying

---

## Summary

The near-term governed-change and inquiry wedge is real but finite. The
venture-scale outcome depends on disciplined expansion after the initial
product proves repeatable commercial demand.
