# MERCURY Investment Thesis

**Date:** 2026-04-02  
**Raise:** $6M seed  
**Audience:** Investors and board-level stakeholders

---

## 1. Thesis

AI is becoming materially involved in trading workflows faster than firms are
building decision-level evidence around it. MERCURY addresses that gap by
providing signed, portable, verifiable records for governed AI trading
workflows.

This is not a bet that every desk becomes fully autonomous immediately. It is a
bet that firms need better evidence, release governance, inquiry handling, and
supervisory infrastructure as AI becomes more influential inside regulated
workflows.

---

## 2. The Problem

When a workflow is questioned, firms struggle to answer:

1. what model or workflow version was active
2. what policy and approvals governed the action
3. what source artifacts support the record
4. how a regulator, client, or internal reviewer can verify the record later
5. what exact release, rollback, or approved export was actually used

Existing categories solve adjacent problems:

- surveillance finds patterns
- TCA analyzes outcomes
- LLM observability traces prompts and tools

None is purpose-built to create portable, signed decision provenance for
governed AI trading workflows.

---

## 3. Why Now

### AI usage is ahead of evidence quality

Firms already use AI for workflow decisions that affect trading outcomes.
Evidence infrastructure has not kept pace.

### Supervisory expectations are tightening

Even without a cryptographic-attestation mandate, regulators and internal
governance teams increasingly care about:

- explainability
- monitoring
- oversight
- control operation
- durable records

### The category has no clear owner

The product opportunity exists in the gap between established systems. That
white space is large enough for a focused company if it ships a concrete wedge
instead of a vague compliance story.

---

## 4. The Product Program

### Initial product

The first product should be understood as:

- AI change-control and release attestation
- supervisory control evidence for approvals, overrides, and exceptions
- inquiry-ready proof and reviewed export packaging
- design-partner-ready deployment for shadow, replay, paper-trading, or
  supervised workflows

### Expansion path

After the initial wedge proves itself, the company can expand into:

- supervised-live productionization of the same workflow
- governance workbench workflows
- downstream archive, review, and surveillance distribution
- assurance suite workflows
- embedded OEM distribution, trust-network services, and companion products
  such as ARC-Wall

That sequencing matters. It reduces engineering burn before demand is proven
and creates a credible commercial wedge.

The current bounded next step in the repo is the embedded OEM lane: one
partner reviewer-workbench bundle over the same qualified assurance,
governance, reviewer, and proof artifacts. That keeps expansion
distribution-facing without turning Mercury into a generic portal, OEM
platform, or broad SDK program.

## 6. Market

The market should be viewed in two layers.

### Near-term wedge

The first market is the subset of firms with:

- real AI influence in a trading workflow
- a change-control, release, or inquiry pain they already feel
- enough budget to pay for a pilot and an annual platform

This is a meaningful but bounded initial market.

### Platform expansion

If MERCURY lands the wedge, the product can expand through:

- additional workflows within landed accounts
- assurance workflows
- governance workflows
- OEM and embedded distribution
- trust-network services
- companion products

That expansion is what supports a venture-scale outcome.

| Stage | Offer | Indicative price |

## 8. Moat

### Existing ARC substrate

The company starts with real protocol and verification infrastructure rather
than a greenfield cryptography project.

### Workflow integration and evidence history

Once MERCURY becomes the record of decision provenance for a workflow, it gains
switching costs beyond those of a dashboard product.

### Publication and verification discipline

If the company executes well on trust distribution, evidence retention, and
verifier packaging, it can offer a stronger evidence story than ordinary logs
or vendor-controlled traces.

### Honest limitation

There is no moat in vague compliance positioning. The moat exists only when the
product becomes operationally useful inside real workflows.

---

## 9. Risks

- market timing may still be early for anything beyond evidence-first
  deployment
- the most engineering-heavy firms may self-build
- the product could be pulled into too many integrations too early
- overclaiming regulation could damage buyer trust
- the evidence wedge may prove real but smaller than the broader platform case

---

## 10. Use of Funds

The seed round should fund:

- the 24-week product program through pilot readiness
- 2-3 paid design-partner pilots
- one funded post-pilot productionization or expansion-track decision
- proof publication and trust-distribution hardening for external review

### Milestones this round should unlock

1. product ready for a design-partner pilot
2. one completed pilot
3. one converted annual platform customer
4. one explicit decision on the next productionization or expansion path

---

## Summary

MERCURY is attractive as a staged product thesis:

- land a governed-change and inquiry wedge that solves a real problem now
- use that wedge to prove ARC's broader rights-and-receipts thesis in finance
- expand into deeper workflow control and companion products only where buyer
  pull is concrete

That is the right structure for a seed-stage company in an early but credible
market.
