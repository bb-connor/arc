# MERCURY TradFi / DeFi Crossover Research

**Date:** 2026-04-02  
**Audience:** Strategy, product, and architecture leads

---

## 1. Why This Adjacency Matters

The same evidence and verification principles that matter in governed AI
trading workflows also matter in on-chain or hybrid workflows:

- agent authorization
- signed workflow records
- retained supporting artifacts
- independent verification

That makes DeFi and hybrid-market workflows a credible long-term adjacency for
the MERCURY and ARC stack.

---

## 2. Where the Crossover Is Real

### Wallet and transaction approval workflows

MERCURY-style evidence can document who or what authorized a wallet action and
which policy or approval state governed it.

### Hybrid execution workflows

Firms that use both centralized and on-chain venues may want one evidence model
for workflow decisions across both environments.

### Partner-facing verification

On-chain environments are already comfortable with cryptographic verification,
which makes them conceptually aligned with MERCURY's proof model.

---

## 3. Why It Is Not a Current Product Priority

The product roadmap is focused on governed AI trading workflows in regulated
institutional environments. DeFi adjacency should follow only after:

- the core evidence platform is proven
- production workflow integrations exist
- there is a clear customer or partner reason to unify TradFi and DeFi evidence

---

## 4. Strategic Implications

The value of this research today is:

- architecture optionality
- language for partner conversations
- awareness that MERCURY's proof model is portable beyond one market structure

It should not distort the current product roadmap or commercial motion.

---

## Summary

TradFi / DeFi crossover is a plausible extension of the MERCURY evidence model,
but it is an adjacency program, not part of the current product build path.
